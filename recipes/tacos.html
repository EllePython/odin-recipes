Tacos Recipe

Description

Birria tacos feature tender, slow-cooked meat (often beef or goat) marinated in a flavorful blend of dried chilies and spices. Served in soft corn tortillas, these tacos come with a rich consommé for dipping. Garnished with onions and cilantro, and sometimes with a crispy cheese crust, birria tacos offer a savory, aromatic, and delightful Mexican culinary experience.

Ingredients

  • Guajillo
  • Ancho
  • Chiles de arbol
  • Beef
  • Beef stock
  • Apple cider vinegar
  • Tomatoes
  • Garlic
  • Oregano
  • Paprika
  • Cumin
  • Bay leaves
  • Thyme
  • Corn tortilla
  • Quesadilla cheese

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 300ºF (150ºC). Season the cubed beef generously with salt and pepper. Heat a large Dutch oven over medium heat, then add the oil and heat through. Working in batches, add the beef and sear until golden brown on all sides, about 8 minutes.
  2. Add all of the seared beef back to the pot and top with the blended chili mixture, onion, beef stock and 2 cups of water. Wrap the bay leaves, thyme add to the pot.
  3. Bring to a boil over medium heat then cover the pot and transfer to the oven. Braise the beef until fork tender, about 3 hours.
  4. Discard the spices. Remove the meat from the braising liquid to a cutting board. Let cool slightly then chop the meat with a cleaver or large knife.
  5. Add to a large mixing bowl with about 2 cups of the braising liquid and ½ cup of the chopped cilantro
  6. Heat a large non-stick skillet over medium heat, Dip the tortilla into the top fat of the braising liquid. Add to the pan and then fry for about 30 seconds.
  7. Serve topped with the beef and onions or to make quesabirria add some of the cheese on top followed by some of the beef. Fold over and continue to cook like a quesadilla, until the cheese melts, about 1 minute. Repeat with remaining tacos and meat
  8. Ladle the consommé into small bowls for dipping. Top both the tacos and consommé with the onion mixture and serve hot with sides of limes and radishes.
